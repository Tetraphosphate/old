Bass Trombone in B Flat

Bass trombones used to be made in the key of G, F, or E flat, or sometimes in other various keys. Modern bass trombones are basically large-bore tenor trombones with two valves. One of those lowers the pitch by a perfect fourth (B flat --> F), the other one by a minor third (B flat --> G). Both valves = key of D flat.

Alto Sackbut Tenor Sackbut Bass Sackbut

There are also double bass sackbuts (also known as great bass sackbuts), but they are currently in hiding. Sackbuts are the ancestors of trombones.

Sometimes people say "saqueboute", "shakbusshes", "seykebuds", "sakbuds", and other (rather strange) variations instead of sackbut. Sackbut is also sometimes spelt "sacbut" and "sackbutt".
